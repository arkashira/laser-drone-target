# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
1-line: GPS-independent drone targeting tool for military and defense organizations using laser-guidance or inertial measurement units.

Alternatives:
1. A GPS-dependent drone targeting tool for civilian use.
2. A drone targeting tool using traditional navigation methods for agricultural purposes.
3. A drone targeting tool for recreational use, focusing on consumer-grade products.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
### Persona 1 - Captain John Smith, Military Operations Officer, Budget: $100,000+
Daily Job: Overseeing drone operations and targeting strategies in military missions.

### Persona 2 - Dr. Jane Doe, Defense Researcher, Budget: $50,000+
Daily Job: Conducting research and development on drone technology for defense applications.

### Persona 3 - Major Alex Johnson, Drone Procurement Officer, Budget: $100,000+
Daily Job: Purchasing and managing drone equipment for military and defense organizations.

## Channels
1. Direct Sales: Targeting military and defense organizations through cold outreach, attending industry events, and leveraging existing relationships. CAC estimate: $5,000 per deal.
2. Partnerships: Collaborating with drone manufacturers and defense technology providers to bundle our product with their offerings. CAC estimate: $2,000 per deal.
3. Online Advertising: Utilizing targeted ads on platforms such as LinkedIn, Google Ads, and industry-specific websites. CAC estimate: $1,500 per lead.

## Content Cadence
Week 1: Launch teaser campaign on social media and industry-specific websites.
Week 2: Publish a whitepaper detailing the benefits and capabilities of our laser-drone-target product.
Week 3: Host a webinar featuring military and defense experts discussing the importance of GPS-independent drone targeting.
Week 4: Release a case study highlighting the success of our product in military missions.
Week 5: Follow-up with leads generated from the teaser campaign and webinar.
Week 6-9: Continue nurturing leads through email marketing and targeted ads.

## Launch Milestones
- D-30: Finalize product development, complete testing, and prepare for launch.
- D-0: Officially launch the product and begin sales and marketing efforts.
- D+30: Evaluate initial sales performance and adjust marketing strategy as needed.
- D+90: Assess product adoption, gather customer feedback, and adjust product roadmap based on feedback.

## Success Metrics
- DAU (Daily Active Users): 50 within the first month, increasing to 100 by the end of the third month.
- $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) target by D+90: $100,000.
